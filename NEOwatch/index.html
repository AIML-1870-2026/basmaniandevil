What am I looking at?

None of the objects shown here are on a collision course with Earth. Risk Watch
Objects monitored by JPL's Sentry impact monitoring system
Sentry is NASA/JPL's automated collision-monitoring system. It continuously scans the asteroid catalog for any future impact possibilities. Every object listed here has a non-zero — but extremely small — calculated probability of impact. Inclusion on this list does not imply danger.

Think of it like a weather forecast for rare events: a 1-in-a-million chance of rain is still technically possible, but you probably won't carry an umbrella. The vast majority of these objects will be ruled out entirely as more observations are made and their orbits are refined. No asteroid currently tracked by Sentry is considered a serious threat.

How to read this table: The Torino Scale is the easiest number to check — if it says 0, you can stop worrying. The Palermo Scale shows how the risk compares to background cosmic noise; negative values mean it is less risky than the average random space rock.
